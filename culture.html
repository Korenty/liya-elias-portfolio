01 · The Highlands

The Gamo
Spirit.

The Gamo people of southern Ethiopia are master weavers of both cloth and community. Liya represents this living cultural heritage — deeply structural, profoundly beautiful — with the same precision she applies to architecture.

"I am the voice of the highland mists and the dignity of a thousand-year weave."

02 · The Sisterhood

Ashenda
Festival.

Celebrated in northern Ethiopia, Ashenda is a women's festival of song, dance, and decorated grass skirts. For Liya, it represents the architectural beauty of feminine strength — structured, rhythmic, and eternally resonant.

"Ashenda is not a festival; it is a declaration. We announce that women are the keepers of time."

03 · Oromo Thanksgiving

Irrecha.

The greatest Oromo thanksgiving ceremony — a celebration of survival, gratitude, and the turning of seasons. Liya honors this tradition as a living bridge between heritage and contemporary expression.

Thousands gather at the shores of Lake Hora to offer prayers, flowers, and thanks. It is the largest annual gathering on the African continent.

04 · The Sacred Ritual

Coffee
Ceremony.

The Ethiopian coffee ceremony is a meditation on hospitality, connection, and time. Three rounds, three intentions: Abol, Tona, Baraka — peace, respect, and blessing.
