Gamo

01 / The Highlands

The Gamo
Spirit.

"I am the voice of the highland mists and the dignity of a thousand-year weave. To represent the Gamo is to wear the geometry of our ancestors — a silent language of resilience and artistic purity."

The Gamo people of southern Ethiopia are master weavers of both cloth and community. Liya represents this living cultural heritage with the same precision she applies to architecture: deeply structural, profoundly beautiful.

"To know your roots is to know your altitude."

The Living Tradition.

02 / The Sisterhood

Ashenda
Festival.

"Ashenda is not a festival; it is a declaration. We announce that women are the keepers of time, the architects of the harvest, the melody of the land."

Celebrated in northern Ethiopia, Ashenda is a women's festival of song, dance, and decorated grass skirts. For Liya, it represents the architectural beauty of feminine strength — structured, rhythmic, and eternally resonant.

03 / The Oromo Thanksgiving

Irrecha.

The Oromo people's greatest thanksgiving ceremony — a celebration of survival, gratitude, and the turning of seasons. Liya honors this tradition as a living bridge between heritage and contemporary expression.

04 / The Sacred Ritual

Coffee
Ceremony.

The Ethiopian coffee ceremony is a meditation on hospitality, connection, and time. Three rounds, three intentions: Abol, Tona, Baraka. Peace, respect, and blessing.
